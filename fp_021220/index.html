Wealth Aware










Welcome to Wealth Aware!

This is a tool to help you understand, more and better, what poverty really is today's world.

For this, we combine a series of visualizations aimed to give you easy, enjoyable access to a large and complex set of data about poverty arond the world.

Here you will be able either to test your current knowledge of poverty with an interactive quiz, or to learn more about the implications of poverty in topics like household income, access to water, levels of hunger, education and finantial inclusion around the world.

And because data is much more than just numbers, you can also watch real life stories of people sharing their experience of living under this conditions.

This site was made to fulfill the requirements of W209: Data Visualization and Communication, as part of the MIDS program at UC Berkeley.

For further information regarding this project, feedback, or anything related to this page, please contact the authors: Priscilla (burityp@berkeley.edu), Anil (ai2084@ischool.berkeley.edu) or Gaby (g.maylagunes@berkeley.edu)

What about education?

Having education helps people think, feel, and behave in a way that contributes to their success, and improves not only their personal satisfaction but also their community.

Learn about the fraction of children at school age that are currently out of scholl by country. You can also find out the gender breakdown of these children.

Financial inclusion is also a key driver for attaining social inclusion.

Financial inclusion helps individuals to make daily payments reliably, access credit which can be invested in their income-generating activities, and also helps people save their cash so that they can make future investments or respond to unforeseeable risks.

Discover the fraction of people that currently do not have a bank account by country. You can also learn about about the reasons why they don't have an acoount.

Data Source: World Bank
